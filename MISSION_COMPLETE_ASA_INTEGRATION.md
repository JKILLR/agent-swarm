## Critical Research Questions Identified

### For Predetermined Embeddings:
1. Can we construct a semantic periodic table with meaningful coordinates?
2. How do we handle polysemy ("bank" as river vs. financial institution)?
3. Can fixed embeddings achieve competitive performance?
4. What's the token-to-coordinate mapping function?

### For Molecular Dynamics:
1. What's the computational cost? (O(n×k×steps) vs. O(n²))
2. How many relaxation steps needed for convergence?
3. Can we guarantee convergence to valid parse?
4. How do we handle ambiguous attachments?

### For Hyperbolic Geometry:
1. Does language structure truly benefit from hyperbolic space?
2. Can it scale to full vocabulary (50K+ tokens)?
3. What's the computational overhead?
4. How do we handle non-hierarchical structures?

### For Semantic Periodic Table:
1. What are the dimensions? (POS, semantic category, valence, abstraction level?)
2. How do we derive coordinates from linguistic theory?
3. Can we achieve >70% coverage of linguistic phenomena?
4. Does position predict selectional restrictions?

## Go/No-Go Criteria (Month 3 Decision)

### GO signals for radical path (need 2 of 4):
1. ✅ Semantic periodic table covers >70% of test vocabulary

3. ✅ Molecular dynamics converges in <10 steps
4. ✅ Computational cost favorable (O(n×k×10) < O(n²) for n>1000)

### NO-GO signals (any 1 kills it):
1. ❌ Polysemy insurmountable for predetermined embeddings
2. ❌ Dynamics requires >100 steps or fails to converge
3. ❌ Computational cost exceeds O(n²) savings
4. ❌ Bonding sites can't handle common linguistic patterns
